01

Preface

During my internship at Roots by Sujata Agrawal in Ahmedabad, I had the opportunity to work closely with a label that beautifully fuses Indian tradition with modern Western aesthetics. This experience allowed me to understand the importance of handcrafted detailing, surface techniques, and the creative process behind party wear design.

Under the guidance of Sujata Agrawal and designer Rumit Prajapati, I gained valuable insights into fabric selection, design development, and the brand's unique approach to modern festive fashion. The supportive team and hands-on learning environment played a key role in enhancing my design skills and industry understanding.

02

Brand Background

Definition — Roots by Sujata Agrawal is a contemporary fashion label that blends the elegance of Indian heritage with the bold appeal of Western fashion. Known for its handcrafted party wear, the brand creates garments with rich textures, intricate detailing, and a fusion of traditional and modern aesthetics.

Ethos — The brand focuses on preserving Indian craftsmanship while adapting it to suit today's fashion-forward generation. From zardozi to sequins, Indian silhouettes to Western cuts, Roots believes in creating contrast-driven designs that are both timeless and trendy — celebrating slow fashion, handwork, and individuality.

History — Founded by Sujata Agrawal and designer Rumit Prajapati, the label began with a passion for Indian textiles and a modern design vision, growing into a promising name for celebration wear that feels rooted yet global.

"Every piece reflects a conscious approach to design, celebrating slow fashion, handwork, and individuality."

04 — Project 01

Party Wear Shirt Design

I developed a series of shirt designs focused on festive and party wear. The designs reflected Roots by Sujata Agrawal's fusion identity, combining Indian surface detailing with modern silhouettes. I contributed to sketching, detailing, and fabric suggestions to align with the brand's aesthetic and client expectations.

Flat sketches — flame, wave, tiger, parrot, snake, crane, sunburst and butterfly motifs developed across ten shirt designs.

Sampled garment — flame motif, silver thread on black cotton poplinStudio photography
05 — Project 02

Customized Printed Fabric for Loungewear

I worked on designing a custom-printed fabric specifically for a client's loungewear order. The process involved understanding the brief, creating motif layouts, selecting colours, and developing a soft, comfortable fabric. This project gave me hands-on experience in client handling.

Three print stories developed for the brief — a branded wine-bottle repeat (in three colourways) and a hookah-lounge repeat.
