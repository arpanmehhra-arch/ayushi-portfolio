Romeo & Juliet — Short Summary

AuthorWilliam Shakespeare
LocationVerona, Italy
EraRenaissance / 16th Century
ThemeLove vs. Hate, Fate, and Sacrifice

Two powerful families — the Montagues and the Capulets — are sworn enemies in Verona. But when Romeo Montague and Juliet Capulet fall in love at first sight, their secret romance leads to tragedy. They marry in secret, but a fight between their families causes Mercutio and Tybalt to die. Romeo is banished, and Juliet fakes her death to escape an arranged marriage. Romeo believes she is truly dead and drinks poison; Juliet wakes, sees him dead, and ends her life too — their deaths finally ending the hatred between their families.

02

Character Map

Montague family — Lord & Lady Montague; Romeo (male protagonist); Benvolio, the peace-making cousin; Balthasar, Romeo's servant; Mercutio, his witty, short-tempered best friend.

Capulet family — Lord & Lady Capulet; Juliet (female protagonist); Tybalt, her aggressive cousin; the Nurse, Juliet's caretaker and confidante; Peter, the Nurse's servant.

Neutral — Friar Laurence (marries the couple secretly), Friar John (fails to deliver the key message), Prince Escalus (ruler of Verona), Paris (Juliet's intended, killed by Romeo).

05

Costume Changes, Character by Character

Romeo — 3 changes. Day (Act 1): light-toned street look. Night (Act 2): softer, romantic drape for the balcony scene. Night/Tragic (Act 5): dark tones and heavy fabric for the graveyard. Fabric moves silk → velvet; colour blue → maroon → black — from soft lover to tragic hero.

Juliet — 5 changes across two costume identities. Innocent Girl (Act 1–2): elegant pastel gown for the ball, then a simpler soft gown for the balcony. Tragic Heroine (Act 3–5): darker post-fight dress, a white "death gown" for the potion scene, and the same white gown worn again symbolically in the tomb — a transition from red passion to pale white as a symbol of purity and sacrifice.

Mercutio — 2 changes. Bright, playful noble outfit for Act 1–2, then a lighter, flexible outfit for the Act 3 fight scene — the costume he dies in.

Nurse — 2 changes. Warm-toned working gown and apron in Act 1–2, then a heavier shawl for the emotional Act 3–4 scenes — always practical, earth-toned fabric representing loyalty.

Tybalt — 1 costume, no change. The same bold red-and-black outfit for every street and duel scene, in heavy brocade or faux leather with a military silhouette — his constancy reflecting his pride and rigidity. He dies early, so there is no transformation.

07

Character Costume Gallery

Reference-driven costume studies for each principal character, developed against 16th-century Italian silhouette conventions.

Romeo — Age 16. Fitted doublet, slim trousers, short cape; velvet or silk with gold trim; sword belt, boots, feathered hat
Juliet — Age 13. Fitted bodice, full skirt, high waist; silk, brocade or velvet with light lace; simpler than adult gowns

Mercutio — short doublet, flared sleeves, brocade or linen blend. Tybalt — structured doublet, sharp shoulders, brocade or faux leather.
