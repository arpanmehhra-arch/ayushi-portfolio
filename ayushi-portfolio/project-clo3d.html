Durbar — The Rathore Court.

A five-look collection capturing the majestic aura of a traditional Rajasthani court scene — developed from architectural motif through to full CLO 3D garment simulation.

01

Concept

This collection captures the majestic aura of The Rathore Court, bringing its royal beauty and rich heritage into a modern design. It is inspired by a traditional Rajasthani court scene, specifically titled "Durbar — The Rathore Court" — an intricate illustration in the style of Indian miniature painting, showcasing a royal assembly. The grand palace, with its detailed carvings, arched windows, and golden accents, serves as the main inspiration for the artwork.

Elements from the architecture — the patterns on the walls, the royal throne, and the intricate decorations — were transformed into stylish fashion details. The design blends traditional elegance with a fresh, contemporary feel, creating a look that is both regal and wearable.

02

Collection Range — 2D Representation

Five looks developed as flat 2D fashion illustrations before moving into 3D simulation — a diamond-lattice slip dress, a crop-and-skirt set, a jumpsuit, a fit-and-flare dress and a fitted trumpet-hem dress, unified by a recurring architectural border motif.

Look 1 – Look 5, 2D representationDigital fashion illustration
03

Collection Range — 3D Representation

Each look was rebuilt as a digitally-simulated 3D garment in CLO 3D, checked front, back and in profile to verify drape, print placement and construction before physical sampling.

04

Motif Development

Source imagery from "V. Durbar — The Rathore Court" was broken down into repeatable motif units — chhatri domes, arched jharokha windows, and jewel-toned border dots — then recombined into four derived motif variations used across the print placement of the collection.

Source architecture → extracted motifs → four derived repeatsMotif development sheet
05

Working Patterns / Printing Patterns

Technical pattern pieces — trouser front and back, bodice with shoulder line and armhole markings, and finished printed panels — were drafted in tandem with the motif development to ensure the border and diamond motifs sat correctly across each seam.

Documents

Full CAD development portfolio — 2D and 3D representation, motif sheets and working patterns.
