01 — About

A B.Des Fashion student building a practice across craft and commerce.

Garment Construction Pattern Making Fashion Illustration Surface Design CAD

I am a Fashion Design student with a strong interest in contemporary fashion, visual storytelling, and innovative garment development. My design approach combines creativity with functionality, allowing me to transform concepts into thoughtfully crafted collections.

Through my internships as a Junior Fashion Designer at Roots by Sujata Agrawal and as a Visual Merchandiser at Pantaloons, I gained hands-on experience in garment development, design research, trend analysis, visual presentation, and understanding customer-focused fashion.

"I enjoy exploring new materials, experimenting with textures, and integrating techniques such as 3D elements into my work."

My goal is to create designs that are both visually impactful and commercially relevant while continuously learning and evolving as a designer — currently seeking opportunities where I can contribute my creativity, technical skills, and passion for fashion within a collaborative design team.

How I Work
03 — Process

From mind map to finished garment.

— 01

Research & Concept

Mind mapping, mood boards and concept notes ground every collection in a clear point of view before a single sketch is drawn.

— 02

Motif & Fabric Development

Translating source material — architecture, textile heritage, character — into original motifs, print repeats and fabric stories.

— 03

Pattern & Construction

Working patterns, size charts and garment construction, built for real production rather than illustration alone.

— 04

CAD & 3D Simulation

2D illustration through to CLO 3D digital draping, used to test silhouette and print placement before physical sampling.

— 05

Styling & Presentation

Final photoshoots, visual merchandising and portfolio documentation that present the work the way a client would see it.
